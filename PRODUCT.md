# Product

## Register

product

## Users
The restaurant owner of Amulya Indian Cuisine (Bellevue, WA). Creates all promotional content personally — no designer, no marketing team. Uses the tool daily to generate buffet menu posters for WhatsApp and social media, needing speed and zero friction.

## Product Purpose
A focused single-task tool: pick the day, meal type, and menu items → get a print-ready branded buffet poster in under 30 seconds. Success = the owner never needs Canva, Photoshop, or a designer for daily menu posts. The poster IS the deliverable.

## Brand Personality
Bold, Festive, Premium. The app should feel like the restaurant itself — confident, celebratory, high-quality. Not a boring utility. A tool worthy of the brand it represents.

## Anti-references
- No fast-food / generic branding: garish colors, cheap fonts, impersonal copy
- No corporate SaaS look: cold blue/white dashboards, generic iconography, enterprise patterns
- No busy / cluttered design: overwhelming decoration, too many competing elements

## Design Principles
1. **The poster IS the product** — the final poster preview must look so good the owner is excited to share it before downloading
2. **Trust through fidelity** — the preview must match the download exactly; any gap breaks confidence in the tool
3. **One task, zero friction** — the owner is busy; every tap should advance the workflow, nothing should block
4. **Confidence over neutrality** — every UI choice should feel intentional and premium, not hedge-your-bets gray
5. **Mobile-first, glanceable** — primary device is iPhone; big touch targets, readable at arm's length in a kitchen

## Accessibility & Inclusion
- Touch-optimised for iOS (owner uses iPhone — primary device, tested with Chrome iOS)
- Text must be readable without glasses at arm's length
- No reliance on color alone to convey information
- Minimum 44px touch targets on interactive elements
